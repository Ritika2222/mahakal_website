Jyotirlingas

Jyotirlingas means (column or pillar of light). The ‘stambha’ symbol represent that there is no beginning or end. When Lord BRAHMA and Lord VISHNU had an argument about who was the supreme god, Lord SHIV appeared as a column of loight and asked each one to find the ends. Neither could do it. It is belived that the place where these columns of light fell is where the jyotirlingas are located. Mahakaleshwar jyotirlinga

Mahakaleshwar jyotirlinga located in the Ujjain city of Madhya Pradesh, India. It is situated on the the banks of the holy river, Shhipra. It is a self- originated linga, so it’s derives power on it’s own. It does not require mantra Shakti for power like the other lingas and statues. It is only jyotirlinga that faces south –‘dakshinmukhi’. All the other jyotirlingas faces east. This is beause the direction of death is delived to be south. As Lord Shiva faces south, it symbolizes that he is master of death. In fact, people worship Mahakaleshwar to prevent untimely death- to enjoy a long life

BHASMA AARTI:-
BHASMA AARTI OF Mahakal is one of the main attraction at Mnhakalesar temple in Ujjain. This uniqe ritual begian every day at 4am. People from all walks of life Ujjain with a desire of beholding this lifetime experience. The eary morning peaceful and calm environment at the temple is wht fills you with immense positivity. In the past, Mahakal Bhasm aarrti was performed with ash from the first funeral pyre cremated at night. But with the changing times, the practice has changed and fresh ash is prepared from cow dung. Since no one is allowed to enter the Garbhagriha, the devotes can witness the enter process through a screen projection installed inside the temple. The process begins with the ‘Adhishek’ or the ritual bath of the shiviling then smearing it with multiple offerings like curd, honey, sandal paste finally cleaning it with milk and water. Further, the shivling is decorated with elaborate arrangments of the fowers and espically the Bel leaves that are considered to be very dear to Lord Shiv are also offered. The process however dosen’t end here. There is more to witness even before the ash ritual is performed. Through the application of various substances, the shivlinga is transformed into a divine face and then further embellished with garland. After this hour-long process of bathing and decoration the idol come the most – awaited and interesting part associated with this first aarti of the day. The entire shivlinga is veiled with a cloth in a manner that the decoration is not distributed. Thereafter, the head priest swings the ash-filled pouch on the shivling. The entire sanctum is filled in the grey dust. This process of sprinkling ash on the shivling last a minute. This is followed by performing Aarti with lit oil lamps, the clangingof bells, and the chants of ‘Om Namah Shivay’ and ‘Har Har Mahadev’. When you are able to witness this entire process and the aarti starts, you just feel that have experienced one of the most beautiful things in life.
